This visualization was found on Reddit, showing the Hunger Games checkouts overtime in Seattle library.

In this case, the visualization is static, showing the data from a 13GB csv with over 100 million rows of checkouts. The data was processed by Reddit user u/Thatshelbs and the graph was made by using Tableau.

The creator of this image uses color hue to represent different categories of things. He uses position in x-axis to represent time, and height(or the span in y-axis, although there is no y-axis in the graph) to represent quantity of the checkouts.

When the creator was trying to make this visualization, his mean purpose was probably to show how the amount of checkouts changed overtime. So he had to come up with different visual channels to represent time and quantity of checkouts. He ended up choosing position to represent time probably because it's the best way to make things clear. And he chose the height of the waveform to represent the amount of checkouts so that readers can easily tell how the amount varied overtime. Maybe he saw some additional information in the csv and suddenly came up with an idea that he should do some comparison between different objects. So he needed a visual channel to represent different objects. Color hue was a good choice because there were not too many objects to be shown, and color hue could just seperate things in a not confusing way.

I personally think this is a good visualization because this graph shows the Hunger Games checkouts in Seattle library pretty clearly. Although I didn't expect any comparison with other objects in the graph because the title didn't say that, these additional information didn't make the graph hard to understand. The legend is clear, and the creator very wisely chose the proper visual channels to represent different kinds of information. Readers can read the visualization intuitively so this visualization is pretty successful.
